You can also prove this result using a little bit of induction and non-commutative algebra.

Given the non-commutative relation of differentiation,\
D times x equals one plus x times D.\
You can substitute one plus x times D into the associative relation of D times x times x to the power of n minus one.

With a little bit of algebra this transforms into an inductive relation between the derivative of x to the power of n and the derivative of x to the power of n minus one,\
This proves that if the solution holds for lower terms then it also holds for higher terms and vice versa.

Plugging one into n gives the correct derivative of 1. Thus, the relation holds for all integers.
